How we describe risk

This is how we describe the risk for flash flooding (surface water), rivers and the sea

Risk Level Chance each year (%)
High Greater than 3.3
Medium Between 1 and 3.3
Low Between 0.1 and 1
Very Low Less than 0.1

For groundwater and reservoirs, we'll tell you if there is a risk.

Flood risk in England and Wales

Flooding:

  • affects 5.9 million properties, equivalent to 1 in 6 homes
  • costs the average home £28,000

You can be affected by flooding, even if you live in a top floor flat.

Impacts of flooding

Help with nationality
We need to know your nationality so we can work out which elections you’re entitled to vote in. If you cannot provide your nationality, you’ll have to send copies of identity documents through the post.

You can still be affected by flooding if you live:

  • on a hill
  • far from a body of water, such as a river or stream

It can impact your:

  • health - through disease or impact on mental health
  • access to clean water and food supplies
  • gas, electricity and drainage
  • doctor’s surgery or hospital
  • transport - cars, buses and trains
  • school, place of work, community centre or place of worship
